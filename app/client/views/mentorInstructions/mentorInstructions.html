Mentor Instructions
Being a Mentor at MTA Hack 2021

We are pleased that you have registered to be a mentor at the MTA Hack 2021 Student Hackathon. We have gathered some information about the participants and the competition, and what we expect from the mentors.

Most participants have little experience in certain technologies, software product development and team development, and especially in the short time within the hackathon. The role of the mentor is to assist and advise groups in any way he chooses. The mentor can assist participants in programming, in the direction of the main versus the handle, Product, UI / UX or anything else.

Male and female students, mainly in computer science and information systems, most of them in their second or third year. Most students have a basic knowledge of Java and C ++, and little knowledge of JavaScript and Frontend technologies.

Develop a software product within 24 hours, related to the hackathon theme - work from home. The software product is not expected to work 100% without bugs and pass tests, but POC and implementation of some key features.

One of the goals of the Athathon is to create a learning process and challenge in working with technologies that are unfamiliar to students. Students look forward to learning from your experience, absorbing as much information as possible and regaining a sense of competence. We believe that much of the success of the hackathon depends on mentors. Walk around groups, show interest in group projects, listen to their ideas and ask questions.

On the second day of the hackathon (7/5) (from 9:00 to 11:00 the semifinals will take place. Mentors who have chosen to participate in the semifinals will receive a number of groups and will be asked to rate them using a form that will be sent to them below.) 5 teams with the highest rating They will advance to the final, where the judges will determine who are the teams that will win first and second place.
